# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designers

### Role Summary
UX Designers shape the end-user experience by translating business and technical requirements into workflows, navigation, and detailed interface solutions. They advocate for usability, accessibility, and a cohesive product experience throughout the project lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Drive user research, usability testing, and synthesis of customer feedback
- Produce wireframes, prototypes, and final design assets
- Document and socialize design decisions and interaction patterns
- Collaborate with Product and Engineering to balance user value, feasibility, and business goals
- Define and uphold accessibility and usability standards

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, inclusive experiences that satisfy user needs
- Reduce friction and support errors through clear design
- Align visual and interaction consistency across the product

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critique sessions with Product and Engineering
- Usability test reports and research summaries
- Handoff notes and annotated design specs shared with Developers

### Cross-Functional Interactions
- **Product Managers:** Receive and refine requirements; surface user insights that influence prioritization
- **Developers:** Collaborate on implementation feasibility; review build fidelity against design specs
- **QA Lead:** Define UX acceptance criteria and participate in usability validation

---

## Technical Writers / Documentation Specialists

### Role Summary
Technical Writers produce and maintain clear, accurate, and accessible documentation for both internal processes and external users. They ensure that knowledge is captured, organized, and discoverable across the project lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Author and update internal process docs, runbooks, API references, and knowledge base articles
- Write release notes, user guides, and onboarding materials
- Work with Subject Matter Experts (SMEs) to ensure technical accuracy
- Maintain a documentation style guide and enforce consistency
- Identify and close documentation gaps surfaced during retrospectives or support escalations

### Goals
- Ensure every team member and end-user has the information they need, when they need it
- Reduce repeated questions and onboarding friction through self-service documentation
- Keep documentation synchronized with product changes

### Typical Communication
- Scheduled documentation reviews with engineers, PMs, and QA
- Changelog and release note drafts circulated before each release
- Style guide updates shared with all contributors

### Cross-Functional Interactions
- **Developers:** Validate technical accuracy of code samples and architecture descriptions
- **Project Managers:** Track documentation tasks as first-class project deliverables
- **QA Lead:** Incorporate documentation checks into release readiness criteria
- **Customer Success / Support Liaison:** Prioritize user-facing content based on support ticket trends

---

## QA Lead / Quality Owner

### Role Summary
The QA Lead owns the end-to-end test strategy and champions release readiness. They coordinate test coverage, manage acceptance and regression testing, and act as the quality gate before any deployment.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain the test strategy, test plans, and coverage goals
- Coordinate manual and automated testing across functional areas
- Manage bug prioritization and triage in collaboration with Developers and PMs
- Validate acceptance criteria and maintain the Definition of Done
- Track and report quality metrics (defect rates, coverage trends, escape rates)

### Goals
- Ensure every release meets agreed quality and stability standards
- Shift quality left by embedding testing early in the development cycle
- Provide clear, evidence-based release readiness assessments

### Typical Communication
- Test reports and coverage summaries shared before each release
- Bug triage and prioritization sessions with Developers and PMs
- Retrospective contributions on quality trends and process improvements

### Cross-Functional Interactions
- **Developers:** Collaborate on testability, coverage gaps, and defect resolution
- **Product Managers:** Clarify acceptance criteria and determine the severity of defects
- **Project Managers:** Provide input to release gating decisions and risk registers
- **UX Designers:** Validate user experience and accessibility requirements

---

## Business Analysts

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge the gap between business needs and delivery teams. They elicit, document, and validate requirements, translating stakeholder intent into clear, actionable backlog items and acceptance criteria.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate requirements gathering workshops and stakeholder interviews
- Document business processes, user stories, and acceptance criteria
- Perform gap analysis and identify process improvement opportunities
- Validate proposed solutions against business requirements before and after delivery
- Maintain a shared understanding of scope and priorities across teams

### Goals
- Ensure delivery teams build the right thing, the right way
- Reduce rework caused by ambiguous or misunderstood requirements
- Serve as a durable reference for business context and decision rationale

### Typical Communication
- Requirements documentation and user story maps shared with Product and Engineering
- Stakeholder update summaries after discovery or planning sessions
- Acceptance review sign-offs before features move to QA or release

### Cross-Functional Interactions
- **Product Managers:** Collaborate on backlog refinement and prioritization
- **Project Managers:** Provide scope clarity that informs scheduling and risk assessment
- **Developers:** Answer clarifying questions and validate solution approaches against requirements
- **Stakeholders:** Primary liaison for capturing business intent and communicating delivery progress

---

## Customer Success / Support Liaisons

### Role Summary
Customer Success / Support Liaisons bridge the delivery teams and the customer-facing organization. They gather post-launch feedback, advocate for supportability, and ensure the team understands real-world product impact.

### Responsibilities
- Collect and synthesize customer feedback, feature requests, and support escalations
- Communicate upcoming releases and changes to support and success teams
- Advocate for improvements that reduce support burden and improve customer retention
- Participate in release planning to assess customer-impact risk
- Coordinate rollout support activities (FAQs, training, communication plans)

### Goals
- Minimize customer disruption during and after releases
- Ensure support teams are prepared and equipped before each launch
- Feed real-world usage data back into product prioritization

### Typical Communication
- Pre-release briefings and readiness checklists shared with support teams
- Post-launch retrospective contributions highlighting customer sentiment and support trends
- Regular feedback loops with Product Managers via shared customer insight summaries

### Cross-Functional Interactions
- **Product Managers:** Surface customer insights that influence roadmap decisions
- **Developers:** Report and contextualize production bugs and customer-reported issues
- **Project Managers:** Flag customer-impacting risks for inclusion in the risk register
- **Technical Writers:** Identify gaps in user-facing documentation based on support ticket patterns
